Technology

Decred uses a hybridized consensus system to strike a balance between miners* and users to create a more robust currency.

Typically, the miners who operate the infrastructure wield considerable influence while the users have relatively little sway. Decred allows users to participate in the project directly without the need for expensive mining hardware.
Learn more about the technology →

Development

Decred fosters a multi-stakeholder development ecosystem that welcomes and empowers participants who want to improve on existing features or build new tools.

Anyone can submit feature proposals, and developers are paid for work to fulfill requirements in full view of the community.  Funding for this work comes directly from the Decred blockchain.

Governance

Decred is constructing a layered governance organization that extends beyond the miners and users to bring forward and represent insider and outsider voices in the community.

Decentralization is a process, and a decentralized ledger is only the first step in that process.

Community and principles

The project is bound by the Decred Constitution, so users can know what to expect: a finite number of coins, decentralized governance and a place to share their views.  Decred's community plays an important role in making decisions, both informally via the forum and formally via the blockchain.

About Decred

Decred is a multi-platform digital currency with support for Windows, Mac and Linux computers. Using simple graphical tools, you'll be sending, receiving and mining Decred in no time! For those who like to tinker, Decred also has a full suite of command line tools that will allow you to customize your experience however you want.

Introduction to the wallet

For Windows users, our Paymetheus wallet will help you get online with Decred in just a few minutes. Send and receive decred, view transaction information and participate in PoS mining with no need to use a command line. Paymetheus is a fully functional, one stop gateway to the world of Decred.

For our Mac and Linux users, a graphical version of the wallet is on its way. In the meantime, our command line tools will get you up and running with complete step-by-step instructions. Advanced users for all platforms can also use these tools to cusomize the Decred experience to their liking.

Send and Recieve Decred

Sending and receiving funds via the Decred network is a breeze. To send funds to someone else, just ask them for an address, enter it into your client along with the amount and hit send. For command line users it's a single command. Receiving funds works the same way. You generate an address from the wallet and give it to the sender. Funds will usually clear in about 5 minutes. There is a small fee to help with the upkeep of the network which is usully much less than what you would pay when using a credit card.

Proof of Work Mining

We want to make sure Decred stays true to its meaning: 'Decentralized Credit'. To do this we've implemented two methods of validating transactions on the block chain. The first is Proof of Work (PoW) mining. Transactions are grouped into blocks and these blocks are validated by PoW miners. When a block is validated it becomes a permanent part of the Decred network.

You can become part of this group by dedicating spare computing resources to verifying transaction on the Decred network. In return, you will be rewarded with decred for verifying blocks. Since it can be very hard to verify a block, you can join a pool of other PoW miners and share the reward when anyone in your pool verifies a block.

Continue Back
Proof of Stake Mining

A second method of verifying transactions on the block chain is through Proof of Stake Mining (PoS). If you don't have spare computing power to dedicate to PoW, then PoS is for you! Use your Decred to purchase tickets on the network. Every block, 5 tickets are randomly chosen to vote on the validity of the previous block. This help secure the network against large mining pools who otherwise might have too much control. Basically it helps keep them honest. Best of all you don't need any hardware to get started with PoS. Pools are also available for PoS to ensure that your tickets are ready to vote whenever they're called. Tickets too expensive? No problem, you can join with up to 31 other users to buy fractions of a ticket and receive part of the voting reward as well.

Planned Features

Part of the reward from new blocks goes toward the development of Decred to ensure that it meets the needs of a modern block chain both now and in the future. These are just some of the features planned for 2017:

  • Hard fork voting
  • A stakeholder-directed DAO
  • Decentralized control of development funds
  • Lightning network support
  • Improved GUI wallets for all major operating systems
  • Enhanced privacy
  • Payment integration

Read about these and more on our blog and welcome to Decred; the future of digital currency.
